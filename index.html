1. My first graph, Count per Genre, answers my boss's question about how many titles per genre. My graph shows the genres on the y axis and the count on the x axis, with the Movie Count printed at end of each bar. My second graph, Average Movie Duration per Year, shows the average duration on the y axis and the year on the x axis. You can change the end date of the chart to add more or fewer years to the chart in total. The overall trend in movie duration averages can be observed looking at the changing bar lines over time. The final graph shows the links between actors who acted in movies together in the year 2020. The reason I chose to limit it to 2020 is because there are many many data points and the network would be a little too cluttered if it had more data. This could be altered in the future, but my graph does answer the question of learning about the cast.

4. I really tried to keep in mind the accessibility of my dashboard as I was creating it. One of the deliberate choices I made was to create a dropdown that a screenreader couldd read through rather than a sliding scale, even though that might have made more sense visually considering my dropdown has a ton of years. Another choice I made was to not use too many colors, since I didn't really need them to convey my meaning. As the readings mention, adding colors can be really helpful, but adding them unnecessarily can make things confusing and a lot of people who are colorblind can miss out on information conveyed by color. One other thing that would have made my second graph more accessible was to add text of the duration (mins) above each bar so that a screenreader could read over the value of each bar. Right now it's just a bar that has to be visually scanned to gain insight from the plot. I don't know if screenreaders can read the mouseover data on the nodes of my network graph, either. I'm not sure how tooltips work for assistive devices, but I did think about this as I was creating my graph.
5. One of the biggest factors that kept me from taking steps for accessibility in this project was time. I was not super familiar with html/js/css/d3 prior to this project, so it took me a lot of time to get used to the tools. I wish that I had more time to look into how to add numbers to my bar chart and captions to my graphs to explain the summary of the information that they show, but unfortunately a combination of inexperience and lack of time led to me omitting these helpful features. I hate to give this as an excuse, especially when in the tech world everything is moving so fast and the excuse is usually a need to push a product out under a time crunch, but I really was trying to keep accessibility in mind from the start.
